Stay Safe from Cyber Fraud тАУ Your Safety is Your Responsibility

1. QR Code Fraud

Money is only deducted when you enter your UPI PIN after scanning a QR code. Fraudsters trick you by saying you'll receive money, but in reality, you're paying them.

Reminder: QR codes are only for sending money, not receiving.

Precautions:
  • Always double-check the recipient before entering your UPI PIN.
  • Simple shop QR codes are usually safe, but never trust unknown links or forwarded QR codes.
  • Never enter your password or PIN unless you're 100% sure.

2. OTP Scams

Scammers impersonate officials and ask for OTPs. Giving them your OTP can result in instant money loss.

Precaution: Never share OTP with anyone.

3. Fake Police Video Calls

Scammers pretend to be police via video calls and threaten you to get money or information.

Precaution: Real police never demand money on video calls. Hang up immediately.

4. Fake Lottery/Prize Scams

Messages like тАЬYou won тВ╣25 lakhsтАЭ are fake and ask for a fee to claim the prize.

Precaution: No prize comes without participation. Don't believe such messages.

5. Hacking Links

Links sent via WhatsApp/SMS may hack your phone if clicked.

Precaution: Never click unknown links, even if sent by a friend.

  • Call the helpline 1930 immediately
  • File a report on cybercrime.gov.in

  • Never share OTPs, PINs, or bank info
